Squat

Movement performed by having the weight on your back, and bending at the hips and knees simultaneously until thighs are parallel with the ground. Then, pushing up using your legs until you're fully stood up.

Bench

Movement performed by having the weight over your chest, bending at your elbows until its touching your chest and then pushing it back up to the original position.

Deadlift

Movement performed by picking up a weight off the floor until your back is fully locked out.

Squat - The hardest movement.

The first exercise you'd do at a powerlifting meet is the squat. This is considered by many the hardest one, simply because if you fail the lift it can cause injuries, due to the weight being across your back. Moreover, as it's the first movement performed in the competition, it can harm your mentality for the rest of the meet. It is of vital importance that you squat weight you can handle, because otherwise you may be dissapointed and not as mentally prepared for the other lifts.

Bench - The upper body movement

The bench is the second exercise in a meet, and it's also the only movement that has upper body muscles as the prime movers. The bench is very non-taxing, it's easy to fail on and doesn't use a lot of weight, so its not very mentally or physically exhausting. A lot of people have bench as their (relatively) strongest lift, so it allows for you to push beyond your limits as it is not dangerous to fail on.

Deadlift - The king of all movements.

The deadlift is the movement that allows you to move the most absolute weight. It is a full body movement, involving effectively every single muscle but primarily your back and hamstrings. It's often called the "king of the gym", as it lets you work with extremely heavy weights - as much as 3 times your bodyweight. It's also really easy to fail on, and the only exercise in a powerlifting meet which you can start yourself, without needing a command from a judge. This gives you lots of time to mentally prepare yourself to move this massive amount of weight.
